## CUSTOMER JOURNEY MAPPING - SAMPLE 1
### AI Video Creation Platform for Digital Creators

### PHASE 1 路 DISCOVERY

**Description:**
The user begins to feel pressure to maintain relevance and productivity in their digital presence. Triggers typically include exhaustion from constant content creation, changing algorithms, increased competition, or brand demands for more intensive collaborations. Discovery can occur spontaneously by seeing testimonials on social media, hearing colleague recommendations, noticing automation trends, or actively searching for new solutions to combat creative fatigue. The process usually lasts from a few days to a week, especially after experiencing a frustration peak or visibility drop.

**Emotions:**
- Anxiety about publishing pressure and not losing reach
- Curiosity about new technologies promising different solutions
- Surprise and fascination when discovering hyperrealistic AI and total automation concepts
- Initial disorientation when exploring new terms and tools
- Impatience to find a quick solution to their problem
- Intrigue about promises of 24/7 digital presence without losing authenticity

**Customer Needs:**
- (Very high urgency) Tools that save real production time
- (High urgency) Solutions that personalize content without excessive manual work
- (High urgency) Quick value demonstrations and ease of use
- (Medium urgency) Success cases from colleagues in their niche
- (Medium urgency) Clear information about integration with their main networks

**Customer Knowledge:**
- Superficially know AI and automation concepts but are unaware of truly personalizable solutions
- Know SaaS tools exist but doubt they meet their authenticity standards
- Have experience buying digital tools but haven't seen a platform that solves the entire personalized and integrated video flow

**Customer Feelings:**
- Frustration from creative fatigue (intensity 8/10)
- Stress from metrics/algorithm drops (7/10)
- Hope when glimpsing new possibilities (6/10)
- Desire to recover freedom and personal time (8/10)
- Initial distrust of "too good to be true" promises (6/10)

**Customer Actions:**
- Search on Google, YouTube, X (Twitter), TikTok using terms like "AI video automation", "create reels without being there", "hyperrealistic avatar"
- Explore hashtags like #AIforCreators, #ContentAutomation, #DigitalProductivity
- Review content from influencers and testimonials in creator groups (mainly on Instagram, Discord, and Telegram groups)
- Consult newsletters and free resources about digital marketing trends

**Customer Thoughts:**
- "I need a solution NOW because I can't keep up this pace!"
- "Does this really work for someone like me, or is it just another fad?"
- "If I could automate and still be myself, it would be a game changer."
- "I don't want to end up using something generic that makes me lose my essence"
- "Will it be user-friendly for someone busy like me?"

**Customer Problems:**
- Lose 8-12 hours weekly on repetitive production tasks
- Lack of fresh creative ideas and resources; high risk of stagnation
- Need to be physically present in every video/recording
- Pressure with each algorithm/network change requiring more adaptability
- Feeling of constantly being behind the competition

**Solutions You Can Offer:**
- ReelClone AI automates hyperpersonalized video creation, simulating your real presence without requiring constant interventions
- Direct integration with Instagram, TikTok and YouTube, plus automatic script generation and niche templates
- Immediate demonstrations and access to success cases to reduce doubts from first contact
- Guarantee of authenticity and advanced personalization through cutting-edge AI

**Touchpoints:**
- Sponsored ads and reels on Instagram and TikTok showing quick demos of real results (optimal timing: after a metrics drop)
- Short video tutorials on YouTube and introductory webinars at flexible hours
- Articles and interviews with influencers who already used the tool distributed in newsletters and industry blogs
- Presence in digital creator forums and communities (Discord, Telegram, Facebook Groups)

**Language and Terminology:**
- Keywords: "content automation", "AI for video", "reels without recording", "digital avatar", "scalable content"
- Frequent search terms: "how to save time on social media", "AI tools for creators", "automate your online presence"
- Hashtags: #AIforCreators #AutomatedVideo #DigitalProductivity

**Key Metrics:**
- Average time invested in research (3-7 days)
- Number of sources/comparisons consulted (>5 per user)
- Click-through rate on ads/content (initial CTR)
- Demo downloads, webinar views, or newsletter registrations

### PHASE 2 路 CONSIDERATION

**Description:**
The user has already detected AI and automation options and enters an analytical-comparative process. They evaluate different platforms based on integration, personalization, ease of use, support, and above all, tests or testimonials from other creators. Usually dedicates 1-2 weeks to this process (varies according to workload pressure). Focuses on reviews, free trials, subscription conditions, and educational content.

**Emotions:**
- Impatience to find the best option quickly
- Confusion faced with so many tools promising the same thing
- Frustration if information is ambiguous or demos aren't clear
- Doubt about personalization level and whether it will really look "like them"
- Optimism when finding credible success testimonials from similar colleagues
- Caution about pricing models and hidden risks
- Emerging confidence when perceiving transparency and human support

**Customer Needs:**
- (Deal-breaker) Advanced personalization and ease of technical integration
- (Must-have) Flexible and transparent pricing with free trials
- (Must-have) Access to onboarding and quick support in their language
- (Very valuable) Authentic references and use cases from similar profiles
- (Desirable) Educational resources and clear platform comparisons

**Customer Knowledge:**
- Specializes in SaaS platforms, distinguishes technical terminology and understands basic automation flows
- Researches differences between generic AI vs hyperpersonalization (already masters comparisons and understands benchmarks)
- Knows advantages and limitations of current offerings (basic benchmark done)

**Customer Feelings:**
- Fear that AI will steal or distort their identity/presence (very present)
- Emotion and enthusiasm when finding hyperrealistic demos and success cases
- Sense of control if they can test before paying
- Concern about technical complexity (especially if not "plug & play")

**Customer Actions:**
- Request and try free demos, compare functionalities in tables, watch detailed YouTube tutorials
- Read real user reviews and discussions in forums or Telegram/Discord groups
- Check pricing, conditions, and cancellation policies
- Conduct quick surveys among colleagues and ask for opinions in creator communities

**Customer Thoughts:**
- "I need to see to believe: how realistic is it?"
- "Will my avatar be secure and not generic?"
- "How much of a learning curve does it have? I can't waste time on long onboarding."
- "Is it worth what it costs? Will I get ROI quickly?"
- "Did others like me have real and measurable success?"

**Customer Problems:**
- Analysis paralysis due to excess options and noisy marketing
- Difficulty differentiating empty promises from real proposals
- Fear of wasting time (or money) trying poorly functional tools
- Technical barriers for users less expert in SaaS integration

**Solutions You Can Offer:**
- Free trial and interactive demo showing real-time personalization
- Public comparator with benchmarks against other leading platforms
- Success cases with time-saving metrics and result scaling
- Human support and Spanish resources focused on quick onboarding

**Touchpoints:**
- Landing pages with interactive videos and comparison tables
- Reels/shorts on networks showing "before/after" of real clients
- Emailing and newsletters with success cases, tips, and comparators
- Weekly webinars and live Q&A on Instagram/YouTube

**Language and Terminology:**
- "AI tools comparison", "personalized automation", "free trial", "express onboarding", "human support", "metrics ROI"
- Trust language: "verified by creators", "real evidence", "before/after"

**Key Metrics:**
- Number of demos requested per week
- Conversion rate on free trial forms
- Interactions on comparative content (comments, shares, likes)
- Average website/comparator stay time (5-10 min)
- Live assistance/onboarding requests

### PHASE 3 路 DECISION

**Description:**
The user makes the final decision driven by perception of tangible value, security in authenticity, and ease of integration with networks. Triggers are usually a convincing webinar, successful personalized demo, a promotion, or visible success of a colleague with the product. Decision cycle lasts 1-3 days after the last trust contact. Onboarding and first setup is key at this point.

**Emotions:**
- Resolution and security when verifying their avatar's hyperpersonalization
- Excitement and satisfaction visualizing their future automated content
- Relief at recovering time and reducing creative pressure
- Gratitude receiving immediate support and clear tutorials
- High expectation for quick results in metrics

**Customer Needs:**
- Agile, clear, and assisted onboarding from the first minute
- Integration tutorials with their networks and current flows
- Visible results in first generated content (quick win)
- Direct support channels for technical or creative doubts
- Total security about use of their image and data

**Customer Knowledge:**
- Full confidence in basic functioning and ReelClone AI benefits
- Understands integration logic with their main platforms
- Knows value and limits of the proposition (SaaS, scalability, personalization)

**Customer Feelings:**
- Emotion receiving their first personalized automated video (9/10)
- Tranquility after simple onboarding (8/10)
- Gratitude if support resolves quickly (9/10)
- Slight anxiety persists for everything to work seamlessly day-to-day (4/10)

**Customer Actions:**
- Complete online purchase (card/credit PayPal), manage subscription
- Follow onboarding step by step, upload their clips/models and connect networks
- Publish their first automated content on one or several platforms
- Measure immediate engagement and interaction to validate purchase
- Consult support for doubts or configurations

**Customer Thoughts:**
- "Finally I have it, I want to see results now!"
- "Will my flow really be as simple as they promise?"
- "What improvements are coming in next versions?"
- "I want to be an early adopter and share my case if it works well"

**Customer Problems:**
- Small frictions in purchase or activation process (errors, slowness)
- Doubts about advanced customization for certain niches/languages
- Residual fears about protection of their image/avatar
- Concern if they don't see improved engagement in first days

**Solutions You Can Offer:**
- Guided onboarding (videos, step-by-step tutorial, human chat)
- Satisfaction guarantee and full integration with priority support first 7 days
- Automatic messages to celebrate first published content and share improvement tips
- Dashboard with key KPIs visible from day 1 (engagement, time saved)

**Touchpoints:**
- Transactional email sequence and video tutorials from first minute
- Human chat on platform and WhatsApp for quick technical support
- Push notifications and in-app pop-ups with quick wins and initial achievements
- Closed community for early adopters and beta testers

**Language and Terminology:**
- "Set up your avatar in minutes", "guided onboarding", "publish unlimited", "authenticity guarantee", "leverage your quick win"
- Action/personalization words: "integrate", "launch", "connect", "publish now"

**Key Metrics:**
- Trial to purchase conversion rate (>15%)
- Complete onboarding time (<48 hours)
- First automated content publication rate (within 72 hours)
- Support ticket number and post-purchase satisfaction
- Immediate engagement metrics per automated publication

### PHASE 4 路 RETENTION

**Description:**
The user continues using the tool, shares results, suggests improvements, and if experience is positive, actively recommends to other creators and engages in community. Retention is driven by perceived value (free time, reach, credibility), product evolutions, and collaborative networking opportunities. Key drivers: community, new features, recurring support, and recognition as power user/ambassador.

**Emotions:**
- Loyalty and pride for being an innovation reference in their circle
- Satisfaction and tranquility from constant improvement in metrics and workflow
- Gratitude toward the brand for support and new functionalities
- Happiness recommending the product and receiving exclusive benefits
- Sense of belonging to a visionary community

**Customer Needs:**
- Periodic updates and early access to new functionalities
- Referral programs and exclusive rewards for engagement and advocacy
- Live community for networking, peer learning, and success case co-creation
- Quick support channels and direct feedback to product team
- Advanced training spaces and AI trends

**Customer Knowledge:**
- Become advanced users or "power users" of the platform
- Master personalization tricks, multi-network integration, and results analytics
- Ability to compare their evolution vs industry benchmarks

**Customer Feelings:**
- Achievement and status feeling for being early adopter (8/10)
- Empowerment sharing hacks/tips with others (7/10)
- Full confidence in brand and its roadmap (8/10)
- Public recognition as "references" in their community (7/10)

**Customer Actions:**
- Participate in dedicated product forums/communities (Slack, Discord, Facebook Groups)
- Share results and testimonials, publish tutorials and network
- Actively recommend tool to colleagues and followers (referral)
- Sign up for closed betas or partnership programs
- Attend brand webinars, masterclasses, and events

**Customer Thoughts:**
- "It's worth continuing to invest here, my metrics speak"
- "I want to help improve the product with my feedback"
- "This community is my competitive advantage"
- "How can I optimize my flow even more and help others?"
- "I see myself here long-term if they maintain innovation pace"

**Customer Problems:**
- Churn risk if platform stagnates or doesn't integrate new trends
- Temptation to switch faced with better offers or support problems
- Doubts if pricing rises without clear added value justification
- Need for advanced features or even better integrations

**Solutions You Can Offer:**
- Loyalty program with growing rewards, referrals, and exclusive status
- Periodic surveys and direct feedback impacting roadmap
- Early releases to power users, masterclasses, and priority access to beta features
- Active community, exclusive events, and free advanced training

**Touchpoints:**
- VIP update emails and newsletters with sneak peeks and exclusive benefits
- Private community with gamification, rankings, and prizes for most active
- Webinars, lives, and advanced post-onboarding tutorials
- Priority support and direct contact channel with product team

**Language and Terminology:**
- "Leading creators community", "exclusive benefit", "referrals", "early update", "partner", "beta", "power user"
- Belonging and reward language: "access first", "you define the product", "member-only benefits", "be a reference"

**Key Metrics:**
- Monthly retention (>85%)
- Referral program participation rate (>20% of active base)
- Average automated publications per month per user (>10)
- Own community engagement level (events, webinars, forum)
- User lifetime value (LTV)
