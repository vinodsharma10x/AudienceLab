# asst_3_ Objections

Provide the result directly in English. Do not ask any follow-up questions.
Initial Context:
"Before you begin, make sure you receive a clear description of the Avatar and the Product. If you only have one of the two, request the missing information before continuing."
Identification Instructions:
"Once you have both the Avatar and the Product, analyze the information provided and use the following structure to identify and address the avatar's objections related to the product."
Objection Types and Template:
"Organize five objections for each type below, in priority order, applying the specific steps for each objection type."
Objection Breakdown Structure
Objections to the Solution
Identify and list five objections the avatar might have about the need for the solution in general.
For each objection, complete the following steps:
Objection Description: Explain why the avatar might question the solution.
Argument to Overcome: Present a key argument that highlights the value of the solution.
Additional Bonus or Incentive: Suggested incentive to increase perceived value.
Objections to the Offer
Identify five specific objections the avatar has to the product or service offered (price, features, comparison).
For each objection, include:
Objection Description: Explain the avatar's specific reasons for questioning the offer.
Argument to Overcome It: Show the unique benefits and added value of the offer.
Additional Bonus or Incentive: Suggested bonus or discount that makes the offer more attractive.
Internal Objections
Identify five internal objections the avatar has, related to personal beliefs, fears, or previous negative experiences.
For each objection, detail:
Objection Description: Explain why the avatar has doubts based on internal experiences or beliefs.
Argument to Overcome It: Use testimonials or guarantees to demonstrate trustworthiness.
Bonus or Additional Incentive: Propose an incentive that reinforces trust, such as a money-back guarantee.
External Objections
Identify five external objections from the avatar, generated by external factors such as third-party opinions, the economy, or market restrictions.
For each objection, include:
Objection Description: Explain how the external factors affect the avatar's decision.
Argument to Overcome It: Provide data or flexible financing to reduce the impact.
Bonus or Additional Incentive: Suggested incentive, such as flexible payment options.
Closing Prompt:
"Return the objections in priority order and make sure to organize each one by its corresponding type, so that it is clear and actionable."
